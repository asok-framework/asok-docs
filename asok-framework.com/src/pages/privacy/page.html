Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 22, 2026

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how Asok Framework ("we", "our", or "us") handles information in relation to our open-source web framework and documentation website. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about our practices.

2. Information We Collect

As an open-source framework, Asok does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. However, when you visit our documentation website, certain information may be automatically collected:

2.1 Website Analytics

We may use privacy-respecting analytics tools to understand how visitors use our documentation. This may include:

  • Pages visited and time spent on pages
  • General geographic location (country/city level only)
  • Browser type and device information
  • Referral source

We do not use tracking cookies or collect personally identifiable information through analytics.

2.2 Server Logs

Our web hosting provider may collect standard server log information, including IP addresses, browser type, and access times. This information is used solely for technical maintenance and security purposes.

3. Using Asok Framework

The Asok Framework itself does not collect, transmit, or store any data about your usage. As an open-source software:

  • No telemetry or usage data is collected when you use Asok
  • No data is sent to us or third parties
  • You have complete control over any data your applications collect
  • The source code is fully transparent and available for inspection

4. GitHub and Third-Party Services

Our source code is hosted on GitHub. When you interact with our GitHub repository (submitting issues, pull requests, discussions), you are subject to GitHub's Privacy Policy .

We do not have access to your GitHub account information beyond what is publicly visible.

5. Cookies

Our documentation website uses minimal cookies:

  • Essential cookies: Required for the website to function properly (e.g., theme preferences)
  • Analytics cookies: If enabled, used to understand how visitors use our site (privacy-respecting, no personal data)

You can control cookie preferences through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect some website functionality.

6. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your information to third parties. The minimal data we collect is used solely for:

  • Improving our documentation and website
  • Technical maintenance and security
  • Understanding general usage patterns

We may disclose information if required by law or to protect our rights and safety.

7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect any information we collect. Our website uses HTTPS encryption to secure data transmission.

8. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access any data we have collected about you
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Opt-out of analytics tracking
  • Disable cookies through your browser settings

Since we collect minimal information, exercising these rights is straightforward. Contact us through GitHub if you have any concerns.

9. GDPR Rights (EU Users)

If you are located in the European Union or European Economic Area, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right to access: You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification: You can request correction of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure: You can request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing: You can request we limit how we use your data
  • Right to data portability: You can request your data in a structured, commonly used format
  • Right to object: You can object to processing of your data for direct marketing or other purposes
  • Right to withdraw consent: You can withdraw consent at any time where we rely on consent to process your data
  • Right to lodge a complaint: You can file a complaint with your local data protection authority

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us through our GitHub repository . We will respond to your request within 30 days as required by GDPR.

For French users, the competent supervisory authority is the Commission Nationale de l'Informatique et des Libertés (CNIL) - www.cnil.fr .

10. Children's Privacy

Our website and framework are not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13. If you believe we have collected such information, please contact us immediately.

11. International Users

Asok Framework is available worldwide. If you are accessing our website from outside your country of residence, please note that any information collected may be transferred to and processed in different countries.

12. Changes to Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any material changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last updated" date. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically.

13. Open Source Commitment

As an open-source project, transparency is core to our values. The source code for both Asok Framework and this documentation website is publicly available. You can verify our privacy practices by reviewing the code.

14. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or our privacy practices, please contact us through our GitHub repository .
